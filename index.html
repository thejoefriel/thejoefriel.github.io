Quick Introduction

Hello! I am a 30-year-old currently living in Bethnal Green with my partner, dog and chinchilla!

Until now I have worked within children’s media, digital/social strategy and most recently was Head of Influencer Marketing in an advertising agency. However, after much (MUCH) thinking, I decided to quite my job and start pursuing what I really want to achieve.

Below I’ve included a little bit of my history, along with two key reasons why I am so excited about the opportunity to study at Founders & Coders, and the required links.

My story

Working first within children’s media and then social media, in particular the world of online influencers (YouTubers, Instagrammers, any social platform with an ‘er’ at the end!), my interests and skills have been in finding new and interesting ways to engage audiences.


Across these few snippets, hopefully you’ll see I’ve always been entrepreneurial and incredibly hard-working, characteristics I know are crucial to make the most of this opportunity at Founders & Coders.

At Fun Kids I looked after day to day operations, business development and online, growing it into a national, multi-platform brand and three times being named in the industry's 30-under-30.

It also means my knowledge of One Direction and Pixar is way too good...
I loved finding exciting ways to communicate with children. Working with the leading virtual world Bin Weevils (basically kids’ equivalent of social media) we built a functioning Fun Kids radio station within the world which won industry awards for innovation and led to our highest ever listening figures in a day.
I became a specialist in helping brands reach audiences through social platforms and online influencers – this could often be hands on such as pretending to be an elf in a video that’s had 2 million views…

If you've got time to kill (or need a break from reviewing applications), click here to watch a bunch of content I created.
During my time at Social Circle I conceived and led the development of a platform that could identify, track and measure influencer activity, becoming a marketplace for influencers and brands.
In my spare time I have done a few simple web based projects, such as setting up the servers and website for DWA.co.uk, a website for Marie Dahlstrom, and also the website while I was at Social Circle.

The ambition / dream / next level...





There are two key areas of where and why I want to go:



Responsibility:



Having spent most of my career working where technology and young people intersect, I have seen first hand how powerful and increasingly integral a role digital plays in our lives. However, I have also seen technology (and the commercialization of online engagement) far outpacing regulation or simple sense-checking of its social impact.

Not wanting to get embroiled in the associated regulations, repeatedly platforms like YouTube and Instagram simply say their platforms aren’t for children, despite Minecraft being the second most-searched for term on YouTube and over half of 10-12 year olds having a social media account despite ‘minimum age’ requirements. This ‘washing of hands’ leaves technology open for mistakes and abuse, from ad automation mistakes to gaming the algorithm as seen with Peppa Pig videos.

I believe there is an increasing need for social responsibility in what we develop – an ethos I know Founders & Coders shares, which I hugely admire. I want to be a part of this, helping shape the online landscape so we maximize the ridiculous number of undoubted benefits it has and will continue to bring.





Freedom:



I love working on innovative ideas and bringing them to life, and nothing lets you do this on such a frequent, powerful basis than coding. Not only can you create something out of nothing simply through your laptop, but it is collaborative beyond anything I’ve experienced in any other work I’ve done. There’s always someone willing to help you or who has a great idea that you can help bring to life too!

Despite leading on web projects – from road-mapping to copywriting – I have always felt frustrated at not being able to get truly involved in the creation myself. This course not only would give me the foundations to do that, but also the network to hopefully find other like-minded people to work with.
